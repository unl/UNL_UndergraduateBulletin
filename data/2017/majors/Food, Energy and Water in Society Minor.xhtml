The Food, Energy and Water in Society minor is designed to offer an education component to all University of Nebraska students that will prepare them with the knowledge and skills to analyze complex, real-world problems from a systems perspective to make informed decisions regarding current and emerging food, energy, and water issues, and the interrelatedness of agriculture, natural resources, and society.

REQUIREMENTS FOR MINOR OFFERED BY DEPARTMENT

A minimum 18 credit hour minor in Food, Energy and Water in Society will include six required courses with 6 hours at the 300 and 400 level.

Required Courses 18

SCIL 101 Science and Decision-Making for a Complex World or NRES 111 Natural Resource Conservation in Society 3

ALEC 108 Food in Society or ALEC 125 Land, Food & People 3

ENSC 110 Energy in Perspective or ENSC 220 Introduction to Energy Systems or ENSC 230 Energy & the Environment: Economics & Policy 3

SCIL 109 Water in Society (AECN 109/NRES 109) 3

SCIL 300 Experiential Learning in Food, Energy & Water Systems I 3

SCIL 400 Experiential Learning in Food, Energy & Water Systems II 3
