DESCRIPTION

The College of Education and Human Sciences offers a minor in International Studies, open to all undergraduate students.

REQUIREMENTS FOR MINOR OFFERED BY DEPARTMENT

Minor–Plan A Requirements – 18 hours

Learning Outcomes

In fulfilling the requirements of the CEHS International Studies Minor, students will achieve the following three learning outcomes.

Outcome 1: Human Ecosystem - Students will be able to articulate the culturally specific ways in which children/youth, families, schools, and communities are viewed and services to them are provided.

Outcome 2: Global Self-Awareness - Students will be able to address significant issues in the world and the relationship of these issues to individual decision-making.

Outcome 3: International Dimensions - Students will understand and be able to articulate how the discipline of their major (or one of their majors) is viewed and practiced in another culture.

9 of 18 credit hours must be from courses with the CEHS prefix or a prefix representing a CEHS department.

Students must take at least 6 credts from the list of courses approved to meet Outcomes 1 and Outcomes 2, students must take at least 1 credit to meet Outcome 3.

Additional courses: In addition to the 7 minimum credits required to meet Outcomes 1, 2, and 3; to complete the overall 18 credits required; students may select from:

  • Any course approved to meet the CEHS International Studies Minor.
  • Any course approved to meet the Global Studies major in the College of Arts and Sciences.
