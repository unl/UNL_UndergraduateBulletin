Global Leadership Minor

COLLEGE: Business Administration

MAJOR: Global Leadership Minor

DEGREE OFFERED: Minor only

HOURS REQUIRED: 12

MINIMUM CUMULATIVE GPA:

MINOR AVAILABLE: Yes

ADVISOR: John Osiri

DESCRIPTION

Chair and Advisor:

Faculty: Ibrayeva (management), Osiri (international programs)

The Global Leadership minor from the College of Business prepares students to be able to identify global opportunities that create, expand, or re-energize organizations and initiatives.

The required courses for this minor will all be offered online.

  • BSAD 320: Global Issues exposes students to human conditions and problems related to development, entrepreneurship, environment, population, and poverty.

  • MNGT 414: Leadership in a Global Context course focuses on international management from a U.S. perspective, and

  • BSAD 420: Global Leadership and the Culture Map is an experiential course where students learn how to launch an international venture or project while developing cultural competency. The project is interdisciplinary and may draw from education, engineering, science, social science, and humanities and is set in a particular cultural context.

    In addition to taking all three aforementioned courses, students are required to earn 3 credit hours while studying abroad on a CBA-approved program. The study abroad trip will provide students excellent global immersion experience and first-hand cultural context in a country of their choice.

    The minor is available to both business and nonbusiness students.

MAJOR REQUIREMENTS

BSAD 320 Global Issues

(Development, population, poverty, and entrepreneurship issues.)

MNGT 414 Leadership in a Global Context (pre-requisite may be waived for Global Leadership Minors)

BSAD 420 Global Leadership & the Culture Map

(Strategies and structures of multinational companies and in-depth focus on cross-cultural dimensions among countries. Taught from an international perspective, not taught from the perspective of U.S. enterprises. Additionally, students will work in teams to propose an international company expansion or launch an international venture.)

BSAD 491, ECON 466 or ECON 467

(Credit associated with a study abroad experience, or similar study abroad credit.)

By completing the 12 credit hours above, a UNL student can earn our Global Leadership minor. The minor is available to all UNL students. Most of the courses for the minor will be offered online.
