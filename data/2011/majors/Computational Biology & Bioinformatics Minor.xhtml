Computational Biology & Bioinformatics Minor

COLLEGE: Arts & Sciences, Agricultural Sciences & Natural Resources, Engineering

MAJOR: Computational Biology & Bioinformatics (Minor only)

DEGREE OFFERED: Minor only

HOURS REQUIRED: 18

MINOR AVAILABLE: Yes

DESCRIPTION

Coordinator: Computer Science and Engineering Department, 256 Avery Hall, Phone: (402) 472-2401, Email: cbb@unl.edu.

Steering Committee: Etsuko Moriyama (SBS), Stephen E. Reichenbach (CSE), Stephen D. Kachman (Statistics), Jeyamkondan Subbiah (BSE)

This interdisciplinary minor prepares students to understand, use, and develop advanced computational methods and tools for processing, visualizing, and analyzing biological data and for modeling biological processes. Studies in computational biology and bioinformatics involve biosciences, computer science, engineering, mathematics, and statistics. Students will be prepared for careers in biomedical, biotechnology, agricultural, pharmaceutical, and engineering fields and for related graduate studies.

REQUIREMENTS FOR MINOR OFFERED BY DEPARTMENT

CHEM 109 General Chemistry I (4 cr) or equivalent

Core courses (13 hours)*

CSCE 155T Computer Science I (3 cr)

CSCE 311 Data Structures & Algorithms for Informatics (3 cr) (starts Spring 2012)

BIOS 237 Basic Application of Bioinformatics (4 cr)

STAT 218 Intro to Statistics or STAT 380 Statistics & Applications (3 cr) (special section of STAT 218 for CBB minor may be offered in Fall 2011)**

*These requirements can be replaced with equivalent courses upon approval except for BIOS 237, which cannot be replaced.

**Students are strongly encouraged to take STAT 218 or STAT 380. However, ELEC 305 (Probability Theory & Intro to Random Processes) can be used to satisfy this requirement subject to approval.
