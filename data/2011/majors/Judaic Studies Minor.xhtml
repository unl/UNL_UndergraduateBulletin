Judaic Studies is an interdisciplinary program of the Norman and Bernice Harris Center for Judaic Studies, 324 Seaton Hall. The minor in Judaic Studies is for students interested in the nature and history of Jewish cultures and peoples, the ways in which Jewish and other traditions have influenced each other, and the origins and effects of anti-Semitism and prejudice. Courses in Judaic Studies are offered by cooperating faculty in arts and sciences college departments. These departments and programs include: classics, English, history, modern languages and literatures, philosophy, political science, psychology, religious studies, and sociology. Language courses in Biblical Hebrew are available.
