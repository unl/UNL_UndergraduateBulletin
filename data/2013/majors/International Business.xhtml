MINOR AVAILABLE: Yes–for CBA students only

CHIEF ADVISER: D’vee Buss

DESCRIPTION

Students in international business study the business practices of other nations and learn how international trade laws affect the world economy. A background in international business will help students become a better member of the management team of any corporation. From banks to manufacturing firms, an increasing number of industries need professionals who understand the world’s business environment. Import and export firms, agricultural commodities firms, and government and non-government agencies may specifically seek international expertise. It is suggested that students begin thinking about where they want to study abroad and their language and culture courses during the freshman year. Upper-level course work and education abroad experience will be done sometime in the junior or senior year.

Because the nature of the major requires studying abroad and careful selection of course work to complement and count for appropriate requirements, students are strongly encouraged to consult with their adviser or a staff member in the Undergraduate Programs Office.

In order for students to be successful in international business after graduation, they should plan to do the following as part of their university experience:

  • Internships related to international business
  • Involvement with international student groups
  • Language study
  • Courses that will provide them with greater awareness and knowledge of global issues
  • Education abroad experiences

Specific Major Requirements

Business Core Advanced–Major (BCA-M)

Requirements for students interested in pursuing the bachelor of science degree with a major in international business will complete 36 hours of course work in three components consisting of:

(1) General Business Requirements (24 hours)

Students interested in pursuing the bachelor of science degree with a major in International Business are to complete 24 hours of 300/400-level business course work for the Business Core Advanced–Major (BCA-M) to be selected from course work offered by:

  • Accounting
  • Economics
  • Finance
  • Management or Supply Chain Management
  • Marketing

As part of the 24 hour requirement:

  • Course work must be from the 300/400-level with the 300-level course work selected from the following approved list.
  • A MINIMUM of 3 hours from each of five departments must be represented.

  • A MINIMUM of 12 hours must be taken at the 400 level.
  • Departmental 399/499s (unless an honors thesis) may NOT be used toward the major.

(2) International Business course work (12 hours)

In addition to the 24 hours identified above, students will complete 12 hours of international business course work. These 12 hours must come from at least two departments. All courses for the 12 hours must be from the Approved International Business Course List found in this Bulletin.

The 12 hours of ‘international business course work’ are SEPARATE from the 24 hours of 300/400-level general business course work (listed above) and these hours cannot be ‘double counted’ between both components. However, there can be double counting between the NBR 6/ACE 9 requirement where, if taken, MNGT 414 or ECON 321 may be used both in the ACE requirement and for the major.

(3) Education Abroad

In addition to completing course work required for the major, students majoring in International Business are also required to participate in a UNL sponsored education abroad experience.

  • This must be completed while in college.
  • A MINIMUM of 3 hours of UNL sponsored or approved education abroad credit must be achieved as part of the education abroad experience. If approved, these hours may fit towards the major, minors or other degree requirements.
  • International students must study in a country other than the U.S. or their home country.

(4) Additional Studies (optional)

While not required, students majoring in international business who wish to seek opportunities for employment abroad should develop a plan early to potentially include the following:

a. Develop a plan of study beginning the freshman year to include Global Studies/International course work as part of the ACE (Achievement Center Education) requirements and other degree program requirements where appropriate;

b. Develop fluency in a minimum of one language other than their native language;

c. International-related internships, international-related volunteer experiences; language partner relationships;

d. Additional education abroad experiences;

e. Conduct research and develop connections with internationally-focused businesses.

Minor Requirements

While not required, students should consider a minor in language and/or consider options in other departments related to the country of interest (i.e., history, political science, global studies, Asian studies, ethnic or European studies, Latin American studies, etc.)

  • Refer to Arts and Science minors for options and requirements which may compliment the geographical area and/or language of interest.
  • Those interested in pursuing a language minor may be able to double count NBR 4–Ace 5. Students will need to compare the requirements for their minor with the ACE course list to determine applicability.
  • In any of these instances, credit hours count only ONCE; student still needs 120 hours for the degree.
  • Be sure to distinguish differences between Plan A & Plan B minors (as Plan A requires completion of only ONE minor, while Plan B requires completion of an additional minor).
