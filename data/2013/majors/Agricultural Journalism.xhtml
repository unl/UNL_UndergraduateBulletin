This degree program is designed to prepare students for careers in mass media communications specifically in agriculture, natural resources and environment disciplines. The degree requirements provide students a broad education combining mass media courses and study in the disciplines mentioned above. Graduates of this program are qualified to pursue careers in journalism and public relations outside of the agriculture and natural resources disciplines.

To complete their mass communications education, students select an area of emphasis in the College of Journalism and Mass Communications from the following sequences: Advertising and Public Relations, Broadcasting, or News-Editorial. Students also select one of three areas of emphasis in the agricultural journalism degree program: 1) Agricultural and Natural Resources Policy, 2) Production Agriculture with a CASNR minor, or 3) Agricultural and Natural Resources Public Relations.

The program’s general education requirements provide students a well-rounded introduction to science, communications, humanities and the social sciences. The agricultural journalism core provides an introduction to the areas of competence required of a professional communicator. The journalism core is offered through the College of Journalism and Mass Communications, which is an accredited program.

Tracks/Options/Concentrations/Emphases Requirements

Student must select one of four options.

Agricultural and Natural Resources Policy Option

Students selecting this option take courses focusing on policy and regulations related to agriculture, natural resources and environment issues. These courses emphasize research skills and an understanding of the promotion of justice in the context of agriculture and natural resources. This emphasis lends itself well to careers in law and policy with nonprofit, governmental and advocacy organizations. Students selecting this option often have an interest in attending law school and should consult with the Exploratory and Pre-Professional Advising Center to ensure requirements for such applications are met during their undergraduate program.

Production Agriculture Option – CASNR Minor

Students selecting this option enroll in one of the established minor programs in CASNR. A minor allows students to combine an interest in an area of agricultural sciences or natural resources with courses in communication to pursue careers in organizations where knowledge of a specific scientific discipline is essential. Students selecting a minor area of study often pursue careers that allow them to combine communication skills with subject-specific skills gained in the minor area.
